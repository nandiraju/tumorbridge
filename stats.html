The implementation of AI-powered virtual tumor boards (VTBs) for oncologists has shown significant potential to improve cancer care. Here are some key statistics highlighting the need for such systems:

  1. Access to Expertise:
  • 66% of rural counties in the United States have no oncologist[7].
  • 56% of Veterans who received cancer care through the VA National TeleOncology service in 2022 lived in rural areas[7].
  1. Efficiency and Scalability:
  • The median turnaround time for personalized reports decreased from 14 days to 4 days over 4 years using a cloud-based VTB platform[6].
  • Case volume increased nearly 2-fold each year with the implementation of a VTB system[6].
  1. Improved Decision-Making:
  • A machine learning algorithm accurately predicted all eight treatment recommendations made by a multidisciplinary tumor board for hepatocellular carcinoma patients[5].
  • The AI model’s predictions were more accurate than those based on published therapeutic guidelines by ESMO and NCCN[5].
  1. Impact on Patient Care:
  • 44% of patients with a confirmed treatment preferred the VTB integrated option in one study[3].
  • Virtual tumor boards improved the accuracy of treatment decision-making for patients with advanced lung cancer and significantly reduced the time required to initiate treatment[10].
  1. Multidisciplinary Collaboration:
  • VTBs typically involve a median of 5 specialists, including neuro-oncologists, neurosurgeons, radiation oncologists, molecular pathologists, and neuroradiologists[2].
  • Doctor attendance increased by 46% during virtual meetings compared to face-to-face tumor boards[10].

These statistics demonstrate the potential of AI-powered VTBs to address challenges in cancer care, particularly in rural and underserved areas, while improving efficiency, decision-making, and patient outcomes.
